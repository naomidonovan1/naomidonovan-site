I've been meaning to start writing publicly for a long time. Between experiments, reading groups, and the usual chaos of a PhD at UCSF, it's easy to let ideas evaporate before they're written down. This blog is my attempt to fix that.

The plan is simple: short posts about things I'm actually working on. That means deep learning models applied to neural recordings, papers I find interesting, small technical notes, and the occasional reflection on research taste. Some posts will be polished. Most will not.

I'm coming at this from a mix of backgrounds — circuits work in the Kanold lab at Johns Hopkins, large-scale simulation at EPFL's Blue Brain Project, and now computational approaches to systems neuroscience at UCSF. One thing all of those taught me is that writing things down early is almost always worth the embarrassment of being wrong in public.

If you're reading this, thanks for stopping by. More soon.
